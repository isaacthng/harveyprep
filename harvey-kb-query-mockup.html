Based on your firm's Commercial Contracts knowledge base, here is the standard indemnification framework for SaaS vendor agreements:

Core Indemnification Provisions
  • Mutual indemnification is the default structure. Each party indemnifies the other for third-party claims arising from (a) breach of representations and warranties, (b) willful misconduct or gross negligence, and (c) violation of applicable law. 1
  • IP indemnification flows one-way from the vendor. The vendor indemnifies the customer against claims that the SaaS platform infringes third-party intellectual property rights, subject to standard carve-outs for customer-provided data and unauthorized modifications. 1 2
  • Data breach indemnification requires the vendor to indemnify for losses arising from unauthorized access to customer data caused by the vendor's failure to maintain agreed-upon security standards. 3
Cap & Liability Structure
  • General indemnification obligations are subject to the overall liability cap (typically 12 months of fees paid or payable). 1
  • Super cap carve-outs apply to IP infringement and data breach indemnification, with exposure capped at 2x the annual fee or $5M (whichever is greater). 2
  • Uncapped obligations are limited to: breach of confidentiality involving willful misconduct, fraud, and violations of applicable law where caps are prohibited. 1
Recent Updates (March 2026)
  • The Q1 2026 revision added a new AI-generated output indemnification provision. Vendors whose platforms use generative AI must indemnify for IP claims arising from AI-generated outputs used by the customer in the ordinary course. 4
  • Updated data breach notification timeline from 72 hours to 48 hours to align with the firm's revised data processing standards. 3 4
  • Added subprocessor indemnification — vendor remains liable for acts and omissions of subprocessors as if they were the vendor's own. 4
